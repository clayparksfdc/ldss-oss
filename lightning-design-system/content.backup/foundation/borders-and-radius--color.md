## Border Color

Borders are a vital part of user interfaces, helping to visually separate, highlight, and structure content. In the Salesforce Lightning Design System (SLDS 2), border colors are carefully chosen to align with the system's visual design, ensuring clarity and usability across all products.

SLDS 2 uses these common colors for borders.

- **Neutral grays** create subtle separation and structure.
- **Dark blue **shows an element has focus.
- **Red** indicates an error state.
- **Yellow** indicates a warning state.
- **Transparent or white **maintains visual balance in lighter or less prominent elements.

To create visually unified designs, these colors are applied consistently across all SLDS components. For example, in Sales Cloud and Service Cloud, a light gray border distinguishes content sections and a bold blue border highlights active elements such as tabs or form fields.

### Where to Use Border Colors

- Separate content using neutral gray borders to divide sections or groups of related content.
- Highlight elements by applying accent colors like blue to indicate interaction states, or dark blue for focus.
- Communicate component states with context-appropriate colors like red for error states or lighter shades for disabled components.
