## About

Borders and radiuses are basic visual design elements that help create clarity, hierarchy, and a consistent look. In the Salesforce Lightning Design System (SLDS), borders and radiuses help create a cohesive look while improving the user experience.

### Why Borders and Radiuses Matter

Borders and radiuses are more than just visual elements. They play an important role in defining spaces, drawing attention to important features, and creating balanced interfaces. In SLDS, the use of borders and radiuses can add these benefits to the user interface.

- Improve clarity by delineating components and sections for better readability and navigation.
- Support hierarchy and differentiate elements to show importance or interactivity.
- Reflect brand identity and maintain a consistent, polished, professional look.
- Foster accessibility by using subtle but effective visual cues to support usability for all users.

#### Theming Considerations

SLDS 2 allows for customizable theming. Make sure that any changes you make to borders or radiuses align with brand guidelines and maintain contrast ratios for accessibility.

## Borders

Borders outline elements and provide structure, serving as visual separators, indicators of interactivity, or highlights for active or selected states. For example, borders can separate content areas like cards and panels, indicate clickable elements like buttons and form fields, or highlight tabs and list items to show that they are active.

### Best Practices for Borders

- Less is Best—Apply borders sparingly to avoid visual clutter.
- Consistency—Use consistent thickness and color across similar elements.
- Subtlety—Use light or subtle borders to create separation without overwhelming the interface.

## Radius

The radius defines how rounded the corners are on elements. Borders have a radius, and container elements such as cards and buttons have a radius too. Rounded corners soften the visual appearance and create a more approachable design style. To keep your interface cohesive, apply a consistent radius across related elements. Elements with dense content, such as buttons and input fields, work better with a smaller radius. Elements that take up more space, such as cards and modals, work well with a larger radius. Don't mix sharp and rounded corners within the same component to keep your design looking polished and cohesive.

### Best Practices for Radius

- Consistency—Apply the same radius to similar elements for a cohesive design style throughout the interface.
- Harmony—Don't mix sharp and rounded corners. Mixing these styles within the same component can create a visually jarring experience and reduce design harmony.
- Branding—Use the radius to reflect the brand's personality. Whether you want the brand to be approachable, professional, or bold, use the SLDS 2 design guidelines to choose the right radius for the elements in your project.
- Usability—Confirm through testing that rounded corners don't detract from clarity or usability, especially for focus and hover states in interactive elements.

Borders and radius are subtle yet powerful tools in a design system. By adhering to these guidelines, you can create interfaces that are both visually appealing and functionally effective.
