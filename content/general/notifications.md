## Guiding Users with Notifications

Notifications are a powerful tool for delivering information. To be effective, a notification must be designed to be actionable and provide a clear path forward that minimizes disruption to a user's workflow.

An effective notification is:

- **Timely:** Delivered at the right moment to offer an opportunity for quick action.
- **Relevant:** Sent through the best channel and in the right context to optimize workflow.
- **Pursuable:** Provides a clear and direct path to action.

## Anatomy of a Notification

For clarity and engagement, each notification needs a few key elements, which create a productive experience for the user.

Each notification must include:

- **Origin: **The identity of the user or system that initiated the communication.
- **Content:** The core message, designed to encourage engagement.
- **Action:** One or more clear actions the recipient can take.
- **Timing:** The time the information was delivered or the time that has passed since an incident or event occurred.

## Notifications vs. Interface Feedback

While they can appear similar, notifications and interface feedback serve different purposes.

- **Interface feedback **is a direct response to a user's action, like a toast confirming a record change. After interface feedback is dismissed, it isn't retrievable.
- **Notifications **are traceable. A user can view, dismiss, and revisit a notification’s content later. They aren’t always a reaction to immediate input and can be triggered by system events or other users**.**

## Distribution Strategy

To maintain user engagement and productivity, balance your notification distribution strategy. With this thoughtful approach, critical information stands out without overwhelming the user.

For an optimal experience, aim for a mix of notification types. A good balance is approximately:

- 30% urgent,
- 45% standard
- 25% discreet
