## About

Illustrations add clarity, personality, and delight to our products. They help communicate complex ideas, reinforce brand identity, and guide users through key moments in their journey.

Illustrations are commonly used in these scenarios.

- **Empty states**—Provide context when a page has no data (for example, when a report hasn’t been created yet).
- **Informational**—Support users as they explore features or learn a new workflow.
- **Error states**—Offer reassurance and guidance when something goes wrong.
Salesforce illustrations are designed to be approachable and inclusive, reflecting the diversity of our users while aligning with the Salesforce brand. Thoughtfully integrated illustrations make our users' lives easier. They enhance understanding, reduce friction, and make for a more engaging and enjoyable experience.

Illustrations enhance your message. To ensure accessibility best practices, add illustrations to enhance the textual content, not replace it. Use illustrations sparingly and align them with the purpose and tone of the screen or message.

### Best Practice

- Use illustrations to soften negative impressions on users. For example, if a user expects to see a certain item but discovers they can't see it, a well-placed illustration can lessen their disappointment.
- Include actionable text along with the illustration to guide the user. For instance, if the user sees an empty list page, include a link in the accompanying text to create a new record.

### When Not to Use

- Don't use illustrations inside related lists or cards. For these, plain inline text is enough.
- Use only one illustration per page. Multiple illustrations can distract users.
- Don't use illustrations as feedback for direct actions. Toasts or popovers are better suited for user feedback.
