## About

Salesforce builds products for a global audience, and accessibility is a core part of that commitment. Countries around the world enforce different accessibility laws and policies, but nearly all align with the **Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)** from the W3C. These guidelines set the global standard for making web content perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust for everyone.

At Salesforce, we use **WCAG 2.1 Level AA** as our baseline for accessibility across products and platforms. This helps ensure that the experiences we design in all Salesforce Products meet both international expectations and legal requirements.

Following WCAG not only supports compliance across regions, but it also helps us create more usable, inclusive, and human-centered experiences for all our users.

### WCAG as the Global Foundation

- **WCAG (currently 2.2, with 3.0 in draft)** is the de facto global benchmark.
- Most countries either directly reference WCAG or have local adaptations closely aligned with it.
- Levels **A, AA, AAA** provide scalable conformance targets.

### Takeaways

- **If you comply with WCAG 2.1 AA or 2.2 AA**, you’re in good shape globally.
- Always check **local laws** and **sector-specific requirements**, such as education, healthcare, or government.
- **Mobile accessibility** and **assistive tech compatibility**, such as screen readers and keyboard navigation, are universally important.
